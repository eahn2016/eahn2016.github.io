Introduction

My Junior Design team chose CHaRM (the Center for Hard to Recycle Materials) for our project. CHaRM is a "permanent drop-off facility that aims to improve our environmental health by encouraging reuse and diverting thousands of pounds of household hazardous waste, bulky trash and other hard to recycle items from Metro-Atlanta landfills and water systems". The employees needed an effective way to track the number of people who visit the recycling facility and the materials they bring in order to receive funding from the city of Atlanta.

The Problem

CHaRM's previous method of recording the materials that were brought in to CHaRM with pen and paper overburdened the small number of staff. With the data from the facility's users, CHaRM would be able to receive more funding from the city of Atlanta in order to open a second facility in another popular location.

User Research

Surveys

The goal of the survey is to learn more about their experience at CHaRM, such as how often they visit and the types of materials they bring with them to recycle. Knowing the process that they go through every time they visit CHaRM will help us to design our app in such a way that makes the entire process easier and more streamlined.
We also wanted to know more about user demographics, such as education level and age. This information is important to know when designing our application because their level of education and age may correlate to how comfortable they are with technology and using apps on their phones. Level of education would also inform our decisions of what facts and statistics about recycling we display on the application. Also, if we can identify a trend between education level and the users who come to recycle, it will help us to better identify our primary users. Survey Results

We found out that the majority of people who recycled at CHaRM went alone. A smaller group of people went with their families.
More than 50% of the people who visits CHaRM go there once or less times a month. This suggested that we build the application so that it is still useful when the users are not at the facility. This lead us to creating a Fun Facts tab.
Around 80% of the people who visit CHaRM use an iOS device. This lead us to focus on creating an iOS application.
From the survey results, the ages of people who visit CHaRM ranged from 25 to 74. This suggested that we create an application that is accessible to all people.

The Solution

My team decided to create an iOS application for CHaRM and its visitors that facilitates this data collection by providing an intuitive user interface for visitors and admin account functionality while providing access to raw data and data visualization features. The application also collects zip code data which will show areas where visitors predominantly travel from. In addition, the application serves as a platform for communication between CHaRM and its visitors, including up-to-date facility closures and information about recycling.
